### üöß CHALLENGES WE RAN INTO

Building Recipe Hunter wasn't easy. Here's what we had to overcome:

**Video Processing Complexity**
Every platform is different. YouTube has transcripts, TikTok has fast-paced edits, Instagram has Reels with music overlays. We had to build a system that handles all of them‚Äîextracting audio, processing video frames, and dealing with age-restricted or private content. Some videos required multiple fallback methods to get the recipe information.

**AI Accuracy Across Languages**
We wanted to preserve recipes in 99 languages‚Äîfrom English to Yoruba to Hausa. Getting AI to accurately transcribe and extract recipes from videos in different languages, especially when chefs speak quickly or use regional cooking terms, was a constant challenge. We spent months refining prompts and testing edge cases.

**Real-Time Processing**
Users expect recipes in 30-60 seconds, but video processing is computationally expensive. We built a background job system with real-time progress tracking so users know exactly what's happening. Balancing speed with accuracy while keeping costs reasonable was tricky.

**Cross-Platform Sync**
Making sure your recipes sync perfectly between your phone, tablet, and computer‚Äîeven when you're offline‚Äîrequired careful architecture. We had to handle conflicts, ensure data integrity, and make the experience seamless across all devices.

**Video Download Reliability**
Platforms change their APIs. Videos get taken down. Some content is region-locked. We built multiple fallback systems and gave users the option to upload videos directly when links fail. The "it just works" experience required a lot of behind-the-scenes engineering.

Building Recipe Hunter taught us valuable lessons:
